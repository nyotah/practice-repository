There are SO many things to see and do in Missoula. Scroll through this page to check the vibes before booking your next trip!

One of Missoula's best features is the beautiful Clark Fork River that runs through the city center.

What to Do on the River:

  • Leisurely kayak through the city or, if you like a little adrenaline, try kayaking or surfing the wave pictured above. Disclaimer: NOT easy!
  • Grab your friends and rent a tube from our local river shop and float the river with your fav bevvy in hand.
  • No time to float? No problem. Choose from a number of serene and beautiful river side spots to pull up a lawn chair and relax.
  • Jump off the the bridge at what is lovingly known as Trash Beach.

Like to Hike? Missoula will be your oasis! Just in the city alone, Missoula boasts over 20 trails. And if you're the adventerous type, there are hundreds more just outside city limits!

Top 3 Most Popular Hiking Trails in Missoula:

  1. Mount Sential - Easily ranking #1 hike in Missoula, Mount Sentinal towers over the city and directly about the University. It is an extremely steep but short hike with amazing views. Trailhead located within city limits on the University of Montana campus. If you reach the top, you'll see the world's biggest "M"!
  2. Waterworks Hill - A mixed angle but moderately easy loop that climbs onto a ridge and overlooks the city. Trailhead located within city limits.
  3. Patte Canyon Trails - A canyon approximately 10 minutes from the city center that features hundreds of intermingling loops and low grade trails, making it the perfect place for a quick and mellow walk.

Take a load off after your hike at one of Missoula's many excellent breweries.

Best Breweries in Missoula:

  • Draught Works Brewery - Pictured above, Draught Works is centrally located and offers a great selection of beer, seltzers, and even selzer cocktails.
  • Kettle House Brewery - Best spot to relax like a local, Kettle house has 2 locations and is universally loved for their laid back atmosphere and excellent beer.
  • Highlander Brewery - Slightly further from city center, Highlander offers a large outdoor space making it the perfect place for families. Pro-trip: get pizza!

Rather come in the winter? Excellent choice.

Just 15 minutes from the city sits Snowbowl Ski Resort. It's a cozy mom-and-pop resort that still keeps up with the big guys, boasting 4 chairs and 950 skiable acres, 39 trails, and a vertical drop of 2,600 feet. Best known by locals for their #1 ranked pizza and bloody marys, Snowbowl is a MUST.


Last but certainly not least... the ledgendary Kettlehouse Ampitheater!

The Kettlehouse Ampitheater is an anomoly in a town so small. Huge artists stop by this stage and concerst run multiple times a week during the spring, summer, and fall months. Head to the show early and stop by Kettlehouse Brewery's second location just next door for a brew.
